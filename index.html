Do you confuse failure as an action with failure as an identity? Too often we cause ourselves mental distress because we see failure as a part of us or a part of someone else. Rather, we need to look at failure as an external action outside of an identity, like stumbling: it happened to us, but we just get back up again. When we make failure a part of our identity, we make it more permanent. Be careful of saying that you’re a failure or someone else is a failure. It’s far better to say, “I failed, but that is in the past” and focus on what you learned from the situation.
